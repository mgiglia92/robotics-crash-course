The Guide to your Raft

Meet your PicoW Raft, Designed and Built for Robotics Crash Course 2023

Board Features:

  • Access to all of the PicoW's GPIO pins
  • Power distribution to power the Pico and any peripherals
  • Screw Terminal for Access to full voltage of power supplied
  • Two switches, one for powering the board and one for powering the screw terminal
  • An easy to access RESET button
  • A reverse polarity protection diode on the input power
  • Two LED's indicting power to both the board and the screw terminal
  • A host of *extras*
    • I2C Breakout for 4 sensors on GPIO Pin 14&15
    • On Board Potentiometer connected to ADC2 (GPIO28)
    • A button that pulls GPIO22 to GND
    • Servo Breakout with level shifters on GPIO16,17,18 to generate 5V PWM signals

Pinout of the Raft

Meet the PicoW

  • Home to the RP2040 Microcontroller
  • The Raft preserves all of its functionality

Raft Provides:

  • Connection to all GPIO pins on the Pico, 3V3 power, and GND next to each pin for ease of adding peripherals.
  • Steady 5V from a Buck converter connected to the input power.
  • Ability to connect ADCREF to 3V3

Powering the Raft

Procedure:

  1. We recommend a 9V Battery to power the board, but up to 12V is allowable
  2. Turn both switches off (off position is towards the Pico for both)
  3. Plug in power supply
  4. Turn on Main Power, LED D1 will turn on, indicating the board is live
  5. Turn on Motor Power, LED D2 will turn on, indicating the screw terminal (and any boards connected to it) are live

More Info:

Some things to keep in mind:

  • Some Rafts do not have operational level shifters, and servo's must be driven by a 3V3 PWM signal, this does not impact the functionality of the servo, but the wiring is a little different. The electrical assembly guide shows how to plug in a servo in the case that the level shifters are not operational
  • Rafts with tested and working level shifters are marked with a sticker by Q1

Board Fabrication:

  • The Rafts were designed in KiCAD and the fabrication files can be found here
  • All v0.3 Rafts were assembled in NYC with Lead-Free solder and solder paste
  • Some Rafts have a different color palette, functionality is not impacted and we consider these boards "limited edition"
  • All v0.3 Rafts have a serial number and detailed notes were taken during the fabrication process, if your raft has a special quirk, there is a record and RCC staff will inform you if your board should have something to keep an eye on
